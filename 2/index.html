A solution of common salt is prepared with 28% of NaCl, to which we call as brine. Brine is led to an ammoniating tower where it mixes with NH3 gas until saturated ammoniated brine is obtained.
Before ammoniation, brine should be free from traces of MgCl2 and CaCl2. This can be done by adding caustic soda which procipitates these impurities as Mg(OH)2 and Ca(OH)2.
